## The Day the Keyword Died (For Real)

Every year Google Marketing Live announces "the end of keywords." Every year the industry ignores it. But 2025 was different. Not because Google declared keywords dead — but because it **built an alternative that works better**.

On May 21, 2025, one day after I/O, Google took the GML stage with over 30 announcements. And the common thread was one: **advertising is migrating from a rules-based model (keywords + CPC) to an intent-based model (AI + agents).**

Its name is **Power Pack**: Performance Max, AI Max for Search, and Demand Gen. Three pillars. Zero mandatory keywords.

---

## AI Max for Search: The End of Keyword Matching as We Know It

AI Max is the most important announcement of GML 2025. It's a *suite* of targeting and creative enhancements that activates with **one click** on existing Search campaigns.

**How it works:**

AI Max uses **broad match + keywordless technology** to expand your campaign reach. But unlike traditional broad match (which still depended on keywords as seeds), AI Max analyzes:

- Your landing pages
- Your existing creatives
- Your historical conversion data
- The context of the user's query

Based on this, it **generates dynamic targeting** that finds relevant queries you would never have thought to target.

**The numbers:**

- Advertisers who activate AI Max see **14% more conversions** at similar CPA/ROAS
- For accounts still using mostly exact match and phrase match, the uplift jumps to **27%**
- One documented case (MyConnect, Australia) showed **16% more conversions** with **2x more ROAS** on net-new queries

**The controls:**

AI Max isn't a black box. It brought:

- **Locations of Interest**: geographic targeting by intent at the ad group level
- **Brand Controls**: granular control over which brands to associate or avoid
- **URL Parameters**: full visibility over search terms, including those generated by keywordless matching
- **Asset Removals**: ability to remove automatically generated assets
- **URL Controls**: specify where the user is directed

The message is clear: Google wants you to give up granular keyword control **in exchange for more reach and performance, while maintaining control over branding and measurement**.

## Power Pack: Performance Max + AI Max + Demand Gen

The "Power Pair" (PMax + Broad Match) from 2024 has evolved into the **Power Pack** — and the difference isn't just in name.

| Product | Function | Pillar |
|---------|----------|--------|
| Performance Max | Full-funnel conversion with 90+ improvements | Control and transparency |
| AI Max for Search | Reach expansion with intelligent targeting | Search growth |
| Demand Gen | Visual demand generation (YouTube, Shorts, Discover, Gmail, Maps) | Visual top/bottom funnel |

The Power Pack **isn't optional**. It's the direction Google is migrating all its ad products toward. If you aren't testing these three formats, you're losing reach.

### Performance Max: The Transparency That Was Missing

PMax received over 90 improvements in the last year, the most important being:

- **Channel Performance Reporting**: finally, granular visibility into where your ads are being shown (Search, YouTube, Discover, Gmail, Maps)
- **Search Terms Reporting**: the same level of detail Search campaigns always had
- **Asset-level Reporting**: metrics per asset (not just per campaign)
- **Exclusion of Interacted Users**: control to avoid overexposure

This addresses the two biggest criticisms PMax faced since launch: **lack of transparency** and **lack of control**. With these reports, PMax becomes auditable — and therefore more trustworthy for accounts with significant budgets.

### Demand Gen: YouTube as a Performance Channel

Demand Gen is no longer "that pretty visual campaign" — it's become a performance engine:

- **60+ launches in the last year**
- **26% YoY increase in conversions per dollar spent**
- **Maps Inventory**: Promoted Pins for in-store traffic
- **Accelerated Checkout**: direct redirect from YouTube view to checkout
- **New Customer Acquisition Goal**: bidding optimized for new customers, with two modes (Value and NCA Only)

The data point that caught my attention most: advertisers using **video + image** in Demand Gen had **20% more conversions**. Format matters. And YouTube is consolidating as a performance channel, not just a branding one.

## Ads in AI Overviews and AI Mode: The New Inventory

This is the announcement that will impact **everyone who buys media on Google**.

Ads now appear:

1. **Inside AI Overviews** (mobile + desktop): contextually integrated into the generative response
2. **In AI Mode**: positioned as a "natural next step" in complex conversations

**Who's eligible:**

- Performance Max
- Shopping campaigns
- Search campaigns with broad match
- AI Max for Search campaigns

**What you DON'T have:**

- Granular control over where your ads appear in these inventories
- Segmented data from these placements (for now)
- Specific opt-out option for AI Overviews vs. AI Mode

Google's strategy is clear: **eligibility is automatic for those using Google's AI solutions**. If you want to appear in these new inventories, adopt AI Max, PMax, and broad match.

For agencies and media professionals, this means **2025 is the year to test AI Max** — not because you want to, but because **not testing means losing access to new, high-intent inventory**.

## Smart Bidding Exploration: The Biggest Bidding Update in a Decade

Smart Bidding Exploration is a **controlled relaxation** of Target ROAS. It allows Google AI to seek out **less obvious but potentially high-performance queries** that were previously excluded by ROAS constraints.

**In practice:**

- You set a primary Target ROAS
- Activate the Exploration toggle
- The system seeks incremental conversions with flexible ROAS
- You gain reach during discovery moments you previously lost

This is especially relevant for brands in **seasonal categories** or **product launches**, where search volume is unpredictable and demand elasticity is high.

## Asset Studio: Google's Canva

Asset Studio unifies all of Google's creative tools in one place within Google Ads:

- **Image-to-video with Veo**: convert product photos into videos with motion
- **AI Outpainting**: expand the frame of existing videos (same technology used at the Las Vegas Sphere)
- **Product-centric Image Generation**: generate lifestyle images of your products in real context
- **Reformat & Extend**: adapt videos to all aspect ratios automatically

What's missing: **fine control**. Asset Studio is powerful for scale, but creatives that require precise art direction will still need human production + specialized tools.

Asset Studio excels at **A/B variations and scale testing**. It isn't (and won't be) a substitute for strategic art direction.

## AI Agents in Google Ads and Analytics

The agentic shift has arrived in Google Ads. Three agents were announced:

### Google Ads Expert
- Assisted campaign creation
- Personalized recommendations based on account data
- Proactive diagnosis and problem correction
- Report and insight generation

### Google Analytics Expert
- Natural language answers about GA4 data
- Automatic trend visualizations
- Tracking implementation troubleshooting

### Marketing Advisor (Chrome)
- Side panel in Chrome with marketing guidance
- Integration with Google Ads, Help Center, GA4, CMS
- Cross-property opportunity identification
- Tagging and implementation issue diagnosis

Marketing Advisor is the most interesting of the three because it's **cross-platform**. It doesn't stay locked inside a Google tool — it navigates the web with the marketing professional, providing context wherever they're working.

## Peak Points: The New Frontier of Context on YouTube

Peak Points is an ad format that uses **Gemini to analyze video content** and identify contextually relevant moments for ad insertion.

Example: in a recipe video, Peak Points inserts the ad for an appliance brand at the exact moment the chef mentions "blender."

This is **much more relevant** than blind pre-rolls or mid-rolls. And it's possible **only because Gemini understands video content at a semantic level**, not just metadata.

## Merchant Center as a Brand Hub

Merchant Center is evolving from a "product data feeder" to a **brand content hub**:

- **Content Hub**: consolidates videos from your site, YouTube, and social media with AI-generated campaign recommendations
- **Brand Profiles**: customizable brand profiles in Search
- **AI Campaign Suggestions**: proactive campaign recommendations based on seasonality and trends (e.g., "Create a Father's Day campaign with these products")

For brands with extensive portfolios, this automates work currently done manually by trade marketing teams.

## What This Means in Practice

### For Traffic Managers and Media Buyers

**1. Mastering AI Max is the new "knowing how to set up a Search campaign."** If you still rely on exact match and granular keyword control, your market value will decline in the next 12 months.

**2. Creativity has become a volume problem.** With Asset Studio generating dozens of variations automatically, the media professional's role is no longer "create the perfect ad" — it's **define the creative strategy and orchestrate the tests**.

**3. Reporting is no longer a task.** With Google Ads Expert and GA4 Expert generating insights automatically, the value lies in **interpreting** the data, not extracting it.

**4. Video is no longer optional.** With video being integrated into Search, Shopping, and all Google surfaces, brands without video production are excluded from growing inventories.

### For Digital Marketing Leaders

**5. The Power Pack isn't a choice — it's the new normal.** Allocate budget to test AI Max in 2025. The 14-27% uplift data is compelling, and the cost of not testing is losing access to AI Overviews and AI Mode.

**6. Agentic marketing is arriving faster than you think.** The Marketing Advisor in Chrome is a preview of what campaign management will look like in 2027: with agents that monitor, diagnose, and apply changes in real time.

**7. Segmentation is migrating from demographic to intentional.** AI Mode, Smart Bidding Exploration, and AI Max don't segment "men aged 25-34 in São Paulo." They segment **real-time intents**. This is better for performance — but requires rethinking how you measure and report results.

## The Verdict

Google Marketing Live 2025 was the most important event for digital advertising since the creation of AdWords. Not because ads changed — but because the **mental model of how advertising works has changed**.

From a model based on:

> "I choose the keywords, I choose the bids, I choose the creatives, I analyze the results"

To:

> "I define the objectives, AI finds the opportunities, AI generates the creatives, AI optimizes the bids, I validate the results"

For marketing professionals, the choice isn't whether to adopt this change. The choice is between **leading the transition** or **being run over by it**.
