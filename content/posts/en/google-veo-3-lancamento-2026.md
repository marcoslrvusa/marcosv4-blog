On February 10, 2026, Google DeepMind launched Veo 3, its third-generation AI video generation model. The leap is significant: native 4K resolution, frame-by-frame control via text, and the ability to maintain character consistency across multiple scenes — something that plagued previous generations of video models.

Google's published benchmarks place Veo 3 ahead of the competition across all major axes. On VBench (the standard AI video quality benchmark), Veo 3 scored 84.7 points versus 76.2 for Sora Turbo (OpenAI), 71.5 for Runway Gen-4, and 68.3 for Pika 2.0. In temporal consistency — the ability to maintain coherent objects and characters across frames — the advantage is even greater: 92.1 points against Sora's 78.4.

## What's New in Veo 3

Three innovations distinguish Veo 3:

1. **Frame-by-frame control**: the user can describe each frame individually or at intervals, enabling granular creative direction. "At 5 seconds, the light shifts to evening orange; at 8 seconds, close-up on the character's face with a surprised expression"
2. **Character consistency**: Veo 3 maintains character appearance throughout the video's duration, even across scenes with different lighting, angles, and backgrounds. This was solved with a visual embedding module that tracks each character as a separate "token" throughout generation
3. **Virtual camera**: the model accepts cinematographic commands — "dolly zoom," "tracking shot," "slow pan" — and executes them with cinematographer-level precision

Veo 3 has been integrated into Google Cloud's Vertex AI and is available via API at US$0.50 per second of generated 4K video (US$30 per minute). For comparison, Sora Turbo costs US$0.20/second at 1080p.

## The Impact on the Production Industry

Veo 3 represents an inflection point for content production. For the first time, output quality is good enough for direct commercial use — not just as preview or reference. Independent studios are already using Veo 3 to produce complete short films, and Google has announced a partnership with A24 to explore AI-generated feature films.

However, inference cost remains high. A 10-minute 4K video costs US$300 in API fees alone — not counting post-production. This makes Veo 3 viable for professional production but prohibitive for individual creators. Competition should drive prices down over the next 12 months.

## What This Means

Veo 3 marks the moment when AI video generation moved from experimental to a legitimate production tool. For marketing and content creation professionals, the message is: start experimenting now. In 18 months, AI-generated videos will be indistinguishable from traditional productions, and costs will continue to fall. Those who master the workflow today will be ahead when the technology democratizes.
