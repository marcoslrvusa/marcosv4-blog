On April 25, 2026, Google expanded Project Genie to support generating navigable 3D worlds from Street View images. Originally announced in 2024 as a 2D world generator for games, Genie now builds complete and coherent three-dimensional environments based on a handful of real street photos. The technology transforms any place on the planet into an explorable 3D scene in minutes.

## How Genie Builds Worlds

The Project Genie pipeline uses three stages. First, a NeRF (Neural Radiance Field) model fed with 5 to 20 Street View images reconstructs the 3D geometry of the scene — buildings, sidewalks, vegetation, signage. Second, a diffusion model conditioned on depth and normal maps completes what is not visible: hidden facades, interiors, high-resolution textures. Third, a world generation model trained on millions of hours of popular game engines fills the environment with dynamic elements — cars, people, variable lighting, weather.

The result is impressive: a walk along Avenida Paulista in São Paulo, generated from 12 Street View photos, produces a real-time navigable environment at 60fps on a laptop with an RTX 4070 GPU. The total pipeline latency is 45 seconds for a 500m x 500m area.

## Applications Beyond Gaming

Google positioned Genie not only as a game development tool (where it reduces realistic scene creation costs by 90%) but as a platform for architecture and urban planning. Architecture firms can use Genie to create electronic mockups of projects from photos of the actual location. City governments can simulate visual impacts of new developments. Logistics companies can generate digital twins of delivery routes.

The corporate metaverse, which had been losing traction, gets a real boost: with Genie, any company can create a navigable 3D replica of its stores, factories, or offices in minutes, not months.

## Limitations and Challenges

The main current limitation is coherence over long distances. Genie works well for blocks and squares, but environments larger than 1km² start showing inconsistencies — streets that do not connect, buildings that change style between regions. Google estimates that the next version (Genie 2.0, expected in 2027) will solve this with world coherence models based on graph transformers.

## What This Means

Project Genie transforms Street View into a 3D world factory. For the indie game market, it is a revolution: small studios can create realistic scenes without million-dollar budgets. For architecture and urban planning, it is a prototyping tool that reduces weeks of work to minutes. The competitive risk for platforms like Unity and Unreal Engine is real — if Google integrates Genie into Google Cloud and offers it as a service, it could disintermediate much of the 3D environment creation pipeline. The deeper implication, however, is that we are moving toward a world where any real place can be instantly digitized, navigated, and modified by AI — with all the privacy and security concerns that entails.
