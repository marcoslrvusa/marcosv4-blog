On February 3, 2026, Anthropic announced the closing of its Series G — US$30 billion in new investments at a US$380 billion valuation. The amount is larger than the GDP of 140 countries and represents the largest private investment round in history, surpassing OpenAI's US$20.5B in 2025. It took just 19 days since the US$350B valuation announcement for the market to re-evaluate the company upward.

The round was led by SoftBank Vision Fund (US$10B), with participation from Google (US$5B, bringing its total investment in Anthropic to US$12B), Mubadala Investment Fund of the United Arab Emirates (US$4B), and a consortium of American and European family offices (US$11B). According to Anthropic, the funds will be deployed across three fronts: expanding computational capacity (60,000 additional GPUs), strategic acquisitions, and global expansion of Claude Code.

## The Logic Behind the Valuation

A US$380B valuation for a company that generated US$1.8B in 2025 (projected US$4.2B in 2026) seems aggressive — a price-to-revenue multiple of 211x on 2025 revenue. But investors are betting on three theses:

1. **Revenue potential**: the global software engineering market is US$600B/year. If Claude Code captures 10% of that market, that's US$60B in annual revenue
2. **Tail effect**: the Claude 4 API is already the most used in high-value enterprise applications (finance, healthcare, legal), with gross margins above 75%
3. **Platform**: Anthropic is building not just models but an AI operating system, with Claude Code (devtools), Claude Enterprise (workflows), and Claude Personal (subscription)

## Pressure on OpenAI and Google

The Series G puts Anthropic in a capital advantage position. With US$30B fresh in the bank, the company now has more liquidity than OpenAI (which has US$18B in cash after its last round). The difference is that Anthropic is spending more disciplinedly: while OpenAI burns US$800M/month on inference and training, Anthropic burns US$450M/month, thanks to its own architectural optimizations.

Google, Anthropic's largest external shareholder, now faces a strategic dilemma: its stake in Anthropic (estimated at 8-10%) has appreciated exponentially, but Google also competes with Anthropic via Gemini. The situation resembles Microsoft's relationship with OpenAI, but with an even more complex dynamic — Google does not have board control over Anthropic.

## What This Means

Anthropic's Series G marks the moment when the AI market ceased to be a bet and became the largest investment thesis in venture capital history. For Brazilian startups, the message is: capital is flowing heavily into AI, but in two layers — infrastructure (where competing with US$30B is impossible) and application (where there is still room for local innovation). The window to build on top of Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google platforms is open, but not for long.
