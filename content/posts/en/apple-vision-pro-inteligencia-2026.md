On February 25, 2026, Apple announced the complete integration of Apple Intelligence into the Vision Pro, transforming the spatial computing headset into a truly intelligent device. A free update via visionOS 3.0, the new version brings a contextual assistant that understands the surrounding environment, objects in the scene, and user intentions — all processed locally on the headset's M4 Ultra chip.

Apple once again proved that its competitive advantage lies not in isolated technological leadership but in the vertical integration of hardware, software, and now AI. While Meta (Quest 4) and Samsung (Galaxy Glasses) still treat headsets as computer peripherals, Apple treated the Vision Pro as a spatial computer that gains superpowers with on-device AI.

## Capabilities of the New Platform

Apple Intelligence on Vision Pro operates across three layers:

1. **Real-time computer vision**: the headset identifies objects, people, text, and contexts around the user. An architect can look at a floor plan and ask "which walls are structural?" — the system segments the image, applies OCR to technical text, and responds with the highlighted elements in the 3D scene

2. **Contextual assistant**: Siri 2.0 with Apple's foundation models (trained internally with the MLX framework) understands spatial references. "Show me important notifications on that screen over there" works — the system maps the gesture, identifies the referenced screen, and projects notifications onto it

3. **Adaptive interfaces**: UI elements dynamically reorganize based on what the user is doing. If you're reading a long document, secondary windows decrease in opacity. If someone approaches, the system reduces immersive content and opens a real-world view window

Everything runs on the M4 Ultra's 32-core Neural Engine, with inference latency below 50ms — no data sent to the cloud. Apple states the vision model has 7B parameters running at 4 bits (occupying only 3.5GB of RAM), an engineering feat for a wearable device.

## Impact on the Device Market

The AI + spatial computing integration solves the Vision Pro's fundamental problem: "what is this for?" With Apple Intelligence, the headset shifts from a media consumption device to a productivity tool. Professionals in architecture, medicine, engineering, and design are the first clear use cases.

Pre-orders after the announcement grew 340% in the first week. Apple reduced the Vision Pro price to US$2,999 (from US$3,499) and projects selling 2 million units in 2026 — still small compared to the iPhone (240M/year), but enough to establish the category.

## What This Means

Apple Intelligence on Vision Pro shows the path of personal computing: context-based interfaces, not command-based. The device understands what you want before you ask. For developers, the message is clear: applications for spatial computing need to be redesigned with AI as a fundamental layer, not an add-on feature. The visionOS 3.0 platform with Apple Intelligence opens a new frontier of interaction design that defines how we will interact with computers in the next decade.
