On January 6, 2026, Meta announced the acquisition of Manus AI for US$2 billion — the largest AI M&A in the company's history, far surpassing the Kustomer purchase (US$1B) in 2020. Manus AI, a startup founded in Shanghai in 2023, built an autonomous agent platform capable of executing complex tasks such as contract analysis, lead prospecting, and enterprise workflow automation without human intervention.

The US$2B valuation shocked the market for two reasons. First, Manus AI had no significant public revenue — it operated in beta with a few thousand enterprise users in Asia. Second, the startup is Chinese, and cross-border technology acquisitions face growing regulatory scrutiny. Meta, however, managed to navigate negotiations with support from its Shanghai office and the Chinese government's green light, which saw the sale as an opportunity to position Chinese technology in the global market through an American big tech company.

## What Manus AI Brings to Meta

Manus AI's platform differentiates itself from traditional assistants (like Siri and Alexa) through its ability to operate long-running agents — tasks that take hours or days to complete, with multiple planning and execution stages. Practical examples include:

- **Due diligence research**: the agent browses websites, extracts financial data, cross-references regulatory databases, and generates a structured report
- **Procurement automation**: the agent negotiates with multiple suppliers simultaneously via chat, compares proposals, and recommends the best option
- **Legal analysis**: the agent reads 200-page contracts, identifies risk clauses, and suggests counter-proposals

For Meta, the acquisition fills a critical gap. The company has invested heavily in generative AI with Llama but lacked an agent product in the market. With Manus, Meta gains not only technology but a team of 120 engineers specialized in autonomous agents — one of the rarest talent pools in the market.

## The Effect on the Agent Market

The acquisition triggered a consolidation wave. The following week, Salesforce announced the purchase of Agent.ai (US$800M), and HubSpot acquired Kandidate AI (US$450M). The average valuation of agent startups jumped 3x compared to 2025.

What becomes evident is that the agent market is no longer experimental. Companies without an agent strategy in 2026 risk falling behind in the next wave of enterprise automation.

## What This Means

Meta's acquisition of Manus AI for US$2B is not just aggressive M&A — it's a signal that autonomous agents are the next frontier of AI, and big techs are willing to pay high premiums to avoid being left out. For the Brazilian market, the message is straightforward: companies that start structuring agent use cases now will be ahead when the technology becomes a commodity in 12 to 18 months.
