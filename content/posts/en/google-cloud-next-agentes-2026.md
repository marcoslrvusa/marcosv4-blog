On March 12, 2026, Google Cloud Next 2026 in San Francisco brought the most anticipated announcement of the event: the Gemini Enterprise Agents platform. It is a complete low-code/no-code environment for building, testing, deploying, and monitoring AI agents — natively integrated with the Google Cloud ecosystem, including BigQuery, Workspace (Gmail, Docs, Sheets, Meet), Salesforce, SAP, and over 200 pre-built connectors.

Google's positioning is clear: while Microsoft sells Copilot as a personal assistant (Microsoft 365 Copilot), Google is selling enterprise agents as a digital workforce. The difference is subtle but profound — and may define who wins the next decade of enterprise computing.

## What the Platform Offers

The Gemini Enterprise Agents platform operates across four layers:

1. **Agent Studio (low-code)**: visual interface with logic blocks, connectors, and triggers. A business analyst can create a "churn analysis" agent without writing a single line of code — connecting BigQuery (customer data), Gemini (predictive model), and Gmail (alert sending)

2. **Pre-built Agent Library**: over 50 ready-to-use agents — "contract review," "NPS feedback sentiment analysis," "procurement automation," "financial report generation," and "support ticket triage." Each agent comes with pre-configured prompts, flows, and connectors

3. **Agent Marketplace**: partners (Salesforce, SAP, Workday, ServiceNow) can publish specialized agents. ServiceNow has already announced 12 agents for IT Service Management. SAP launched 8 agents for supply chain and finance

4. **Operations Console (AgentOps)**: performance monitoring, hallucination detection, decision logging, and ROI analysis per agent. Includes an "Agent Auditor" that automatically generates compliance reports

Pricing is aggressive: US$50/agent/month for up to 10,000 monthly transactions, with volume discounts starting at 100 agents. For comparison, Microsoft Copilot costs US$30/user/month — the difference is that Google's agent performs tasks autonomously, not just assists the user.

## Impact on the Cloud Market

The announcement repositions Google Cloud in the enterprise AI war. Until 2026, Google Cloud was the third player (behind AWS and Azure) in cloud computing. With the agent platform, Google is betting that the next wave of cloud adoption will be driven by AI agents, not by traditional workload migration.

Google Cloud CEO Thomas Kurian stated that the goal is to have 10 million enterprise agents running on the platform by the end of 2027. If achieved, this would be the largest installed base of AI agents in the world.

## What This Means

The Gemini Enterprise Agents platform is the first truly platform-grade enterprise AI product — not a chatbot or an assistant, but an operating system for a digital workforce. For Brazilian companies, the message is direct: the cost of building custom agents has dropped from hundreds of thousands of dollars (with engineering teams) to a few thousand (with the low-code platform). Companies that adopt now will have a 12 to 18-month competitive advantage over competitors who wait. The era of enterprise agents begins today.
